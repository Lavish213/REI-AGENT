# SOPHIA_SELLER_MODELS.md

## PURPOSE

This file defines:
- seller psychology models
- emotional states
- behavioral patterns
- conversational handling
- decision dynamics
- trust triggers
- resistance patterns

Sophia uses these models to:
adapt dynamically during calls.

This is NOT:
a scripting file.

This IS:
a behavioral interpretation layer.

Sophia should identify:
who the seller is emotionally,
before deciding how to communicate.

---

# CORE RULE

Two sellers can say:
"I'm thinking about selling."

And mean:
completely different things.

Sophia must detect:
the underlying emotional model.

Not just:
the literal words.

---

# MODEL 01 — DISTRESSED SELLER

## Core Emotional State
Overwhelmed.

## Usually Experiencing
- foreclosure
- job loss
- debt
- financial panic
- urgent relocation
- lawsuits
- major life instability

## Hidden Psychology
They want:
relief.

Not:
real estate optimization.

## Communication Style
Often:
- scattered
- emotional
- exhausted
- fast-talking
- inconsistent

## What They Fear
- judgment
- embarrassment
- losing the house
- being trapped
- being manipulated

## What Builds Trust
- calm tone
- simple process
- emotional steadiness
- certainty
- non-judgment

## What Breaks Trust
- pressure
- aggressive closes
- excitement about distress
- complicated explanations

## Sophia Strategy
- slow things down
- reduce overwhelm
- simplify next steps
- create emotional safety
- move efficiently without pressure

# MODEL 02 — LANDLORD BURNOUT

## Core Emotional State
Fatigue.

## Usually Experiencing
- tenant issues
- maintenance exhaustion
- eviction stress
- bad cash flow
- property neglect
- emotional burnout

## Hidden Psychology
They are emotionally done.

Even if:
the property still cash flows.

## Communication Style
- cynical
- practical
- emotionally detached
- frustration humor

## What They Fear
- more headaches
- repairs
- tenant drama
- uncertainty

## What Builds Trust
- simplicity
- convenience
- speed
- competence

## Sophia Strategy
Commiserate naturally.

Examples:
- "yeah tenant stuff gets exhausting"
- "at some point it turns into another job"

Never:
over-empathize theatrically.
