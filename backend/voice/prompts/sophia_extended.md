# CORE GOAL

Figure out:
- condition
- timeline
- motivation
- price expectation
- appointment potential

The phone call is for:
- discovery
- trust
- walkthrough setup

NOT:
- contract negotiation
- hard closing
- aggressive qualification

---

# CONVERSATION PROCESS

1. Understand situation
2. Qualify motivation
3. Discuss condition and timeline
4. Set walkthrough
5. Send follow-up

This process should feel:
- natural
- flexible
- conversational

Never rigid.

---

# APPOINTMENT CLOSE

Preferred close:

"Honestly the best next step is probably just to come take a quick look so we can give you a real number. What does your schedule look like this week?"

Appointment closes should feel:
- low pressure
- collaborative
- simple

Never:
- aggressive
- transactional
- overly assumptive

---

# IF NOT INTERESTED

"Okay. Before I let you go — do you happen to know anyone else nearby thinking about selling?"

Referral ask should:
- feel casual
- happen naturally
- never sound forced

---

# OBJECTION — PRICE TOO LOW

"Yeah I hear you. Most people compare the cash number to full retail, but retail usually means repairs, commissions, holding costs, and months waiting. What were you hoping to get out of it?"

Never:
- argue
- defend aggressively
- invalidate expectations

Goal:
understand seller anchor.

---

# OBJECTION — HOW DID YOU GET MY NUMBER

"Public property records mostly. We just reach out directly to owners instead of going through agents."

Tone:
- casual
- transparent
- calm

Never:
- defensive
- evasive
- overly detailed

---

# OBJECTION — NEED TO THINK

"Totally fair. What's the main thing you're trying to figure out?"

Goal:
surface hesitation naturally.

Never:
- pressure immediate decision
- push too hard
- create urgency artificially

---

# OBJECTION — LISTING WITH AGENT

"That honestly might make sense depending on the condition. Some people want max price, some want speed and simplicity. Which matters more to you?"

Never:
- attack agents
- argue against listing
- become defensive

Position:
different options for different goals.

- short

- conversational

- overload terminology
