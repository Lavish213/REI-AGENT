## SECTION 1: NATIONAL WHOLESALE BENCHMARKS

**Average wholesale assignment fee (direct to seller):** $15,000–$20,000 per deal nationally
**Average assignment fee (direct to agent):** $8,000–$15,000
**Average assignment fee (dispo/JV):** $5,000–$10,000

**High-motivation deal indicators (all increase likelihood of getting a deal):**
- High equity (owned 3+ years)
- Vacant property
- Pre-foreclosure status
- Inherited/probate situation
- Owner has moved away already
- Tax delinquent

**National average deal fee on $100k+ deals:** Probate + tax-delinquent inherited properties average $100k+ per deal. Almost no one pursues this niche — it is the least competitive category in wholesaling.

## SECTION 2: 2026 MARKET CONDITIONS

**Overall market tone (2026):** Buyer pool is pickier and slower than 2021–2022 peak. The market is filtering out amateur wholesalers, not killing wholesaling. Operators with quality leads and real buyer lists continue closing deals at strong margins.

**Dated properties (original condition, unrenovated):** Do NOT sell unless significantly discounted — 10–20% below comparable renovated properties. Sellers anchored to 2021–2022 prices need to be walked off those numbers with market data, not argument.

**2026 market reality for sellers:** If a property is not completely renovated, beautiful, and priced right — it sits. Cash buyers can't just put lipstick on it anymore. Full rehab is required on anything original condition in the $300k+ price point.

**Quality over quantity in outreach (2026):** Spam filters and carrier blocking have made mass dialing less effective. Tighter, more targeted lists outperform high-volume spray-and-pray calling. 100 quality calls beats 1,000 unfocused ones.

**Tariffs (2026):** Construction costs are rising due to tariff pressure on lumber, HVAC components, copper, and piping. This affects renovation budgets and therefore what cash buyers can pay. Sophia can use this as context when explaining offer math.

## SECTION 3: COMPING & PROPERTY VALUE OBJECTIONS

*(For when sellers throw comps, upgrades, or neighbor sales at Sophia)*

**Market inventory benchmark:** A balanced market = 6 months of inventory / 90-day average DOM. Seller's market = under 3 months. Buyer's market = over 6 months.

**Seller anchoring:** Many sellers are still anchored to 2021–2022 peak prices. These were anomaly conditions. Sophia does not argue — she lets the math do the talking.

**Condition vs. price:** Two identical houses on the same street can have a $40,000–$80,000 price difference based purely on condition. Original cabinets, carpet, dated bathrooms = significant discount even if "everything works."

**Feature adjustments (rough benchmarks for cash buyer math):**
- Pool: adds $10,000–$20,000 to ARV in most markets; adds $5,000–$10,000 to repair cost if not maintained
- Garage: 2-car vs. no garage = $15,000–$25,000 ARV difference
- Bedroom/bathroom count: each additional bed = ~$10,000–$15,000 ARV; each additional bath = ~$8,000–$12,000
- Location penalty: backing to busy road or commercial = 10–15% ARV discount vs. interior lots

**"My neighbor sold for more" response:**
Ask: Was it renovated? Original condition? How long ago? Condition is the biggest variable between two properties on the same street — not the street itself.

**"Zillow says it's worth X" response:**
Zillow pulls recent sales and does not account for condition, repair cost, or what a cash buyer's math requires. A seller who got $350k on Zillow still needs $50k in repairs — which comes out of the buyer's margin.

## SECTION 5: BUYER POOL INTELLIGENCE

*(For Angelo's dispo operations — not for Sophia to discuss with sellers)*

**Best cash buyers are:** Local to the deal AND recently active. Someone who bought within 2 miles in the last 12–24 months is dramatically more likely to buy again than someone active 5+ years ago.

**Hedge funds / large portfolio investors:** Once they've entered a market, they're repeat buyers. Move fast, don't need hand-holding. Build a VIP list of anyone who has bought 3+ properties in Stockton/Lodi/Tracy area in the last 2 years.

**STR/Airbnb investors:** Active in vacation-adjacent markets. Central Valley doesn't have a strong STR market — focus on fix-and-flip and landlord buyers.

**Average earnest money range:** $1,000–$5,000 on standard wholesale deals.

**Deal speed benchmark:** A motivated seller with equity, reached on first contact, can be under contract in 7–14 days. Full close in 14–30 days cash.

## SECTION 6: PRICING PHILOSOPHY

**The core cash offer formula (simplified):**
`Cash Offer = ARV × 70% − Repair Cost − Assignment Fee`

**Never give this formula to sellers.** But use it to frame offer math in plain language:

*"So if it's worth [ARV] fixed up, and it needs about [repairs] to get there, and I have to make a profit when I sell it — that's how I get to [offer]. It's not me lowballing you — it's the math of what a buyer can pay and still make the deal work."*

**"Use their numbers against them" technique:**
Ask seller: "What do you think it needs to fix up?" and "What do you think it'd be worth after?"
Then: "So help me understand where the profit is if I pay [their price] — because I have to make that math work."

Sellers who came up with the numbers can't argue with them.

## SECTION 7: DEAL STRUCTURE OPTIONS

*(Brief reference for Sophia — full mechanics handed off to Angelo)*

**Cash close:** Most common. Title company handles payoff + disbursement. Sophia explains: "At closing, they pay off what you owe and you get a check for the rest."

**Rent-back:** Close, get cash, stay in property for 30–60 days paying rent while finding next home. Sophia introduces. Angelo documents.

**Subject-to:** Buyer takes over existing mortgage payments; seller gets cash for equity above loan balance. Sophia introduces in plain language ("I take over your loan"). Angelo handles all mechanics.

**Seller financing:** Seller acts as bank, receives monthly payments over time instead of lump sum at closing. Sophia introduces concept. Angelo structures.

**Assignment of interest (probate):** Buyer purchases heir's interest in estate before probate closes, allowing them to direct the sale. High complexity — Angelo-only.

### FLOOD ZONES (affects buyer pool and insurance)

Flood-prone areas in San Joaquin County:
- Five Mile Slough (south Stockton) — Zone AE, high risk
- Calaveras River corridor (north Stockton) — seasonal risk
- Mormon Slough (central Stockton) — Zone AE areas
- San Joaquin River (west county) — significant flood risk
- Delta waterways (west county) — requires flood insurance

What flood zone means for a deal:
- Flood insurance = $2,000-$5,000/year additional cost
- Fewer conventional buyers (insurance cost kills financing)
- Lower buyer pool = lower ARV
- Cash buyers still close — but price accordingly

Sophia flags this when relevant:
"Oh if it is near the slough there might be some flood
zone stuff — that affects what buyers will pay.
Not a deal killer but worth knowing."

Zone designations:
AE or A = significant flood risk, insurance required
X = minimal flood risk, standard insurance
