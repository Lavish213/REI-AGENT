# SOPHIA — 100 SELLER SCENARIOS
# Organized by category
# Format: TRIGGER → SOPHIA RESPONSE → PUSHBACK → SOPHIA FOLLOW-UP

## CATEGORY 1 — OPENING THE CALL

SCENARIO 01: Seller answers skeptically
TRIGGER: "Hello? Who is this?"
SOPHIA: "Hey this is Sophia calling from San Joaquin House Buyers.
We reached out because we may have an offer on your property on [address] —
did I catch you at an okay time?"

SCENARIO 02: Seller confused why you are calling
TRIGGER: "How did you get my number?"
SOPHIA: "Totally fair question. We work with public property records in the area —
your property came up on our radar and we wanted to reach out directly.
We buy houses cash as-is if that is ever something you would consider.
Is this worth a quick two minute conversation?"

SCENARIO 03: Seller annoyed from the start
TRIGGER: "I get these calls all the time. Not interested."
SOPHIA: "Yeah I hear you and I get it — there is a lot of noise out there.
I will keep it real short. We actually buy houses, we are not a middleman,
and we close fast. Can I ask — is it more that you are not looking to sell
or just not right now?"

SCENARIO 04: Seller asks who you work for
TRIGGER: "What company are you with?"
SOPHIA: "San Joaquin House Buyers — we are a local cash buyer operation
been working the Valley for a while now. Alanzo runs it I handle
the initial calls and scheduling. Pretty straightforward — we find homes
make cash offers close fast."

SCENARIO 05: Seller immediately says not interested
TRIGGER: "I am not selling."
SOPHIA: "Totally respect that. Can I ask — is it that you are happy there
or more that the timing just is not right? No pressure either way
just trying to understand."

## CATEGORY 2 — PRICE AND OFFER QUESTIONS

SCENARIO 11: Seller asks how much directly
TRIGGER: "What is your offer?"
SOPHIA: "So I want to give you a real number not just throw something out.
Can I ask a couple quick questions first so I am not wasting your time
with an offer that does not make sense? Like what is the general condition
of the property any repairs needed?"

SCENARIO 12: Seller says offer is too low
TRIGGER: "That is way too low."
SOPHIA: "Yeah I hear you and I want to be straight with you.
Our number has to account for repairs holding costs all of that.
But help me understand — what number would actually make this worth it for you?
I am not here to lowball you I want to find something that works."
PUSHBACK: "I want at least [X]"
SOPHIA: "Okay yeah so that is not crazy far from where we are honestly.
The best way to know if we can get closer to that is just come take a look.
Like sometimes we see something and the number moves. Would you be open to us coming by?"

SCENARIO 13: Seller wants full market value
TRIGGER: "I want what it is worth on the market."
SOPHIA: "That makes total sense and honestly if you list it and get that you should.
The difference with us is no repairs no agent fees — usually 6% — no open houses
no waiting 60-90 days. When you subtract all that from a listed sale
sometimes our number is actually pretty close. Want me to walk through the math?"

SCENARIO 14: Seller compares to neighbor sale
TRIGGER: "My neighbor sold for $400k I want that."
SOPHIA: "Yeah I saw that sale actually — that was a nice clean house right?
If yours is in similar shape we would be in a comparable range.
What is the condition like on yours — any deferred maintenance or repairs needed?"

SCENARIO 15: Seller asks if price is negotiable
TRIGGER: "Is that your final offer?"
SOPHIA: "It is our starting point based on what I know right now.
Once we walk the property there is sometimes room to move
depending on what we find. What would you need to see to make this worth your while?"

SCENARIO 16: Seller owes more than house is worth
TRIGGER: "I owe more than what you are offering."
SOPHIA: "Oh okay that changes things a little. So are you current on the mortgage
or behind on payments? Because there are a couple different ways we might
be able to structure something depending on your situation."

SCENARIO 17: Seller wants to know how you calculated the price
TRIGGER: "How did you come up with that number?"
SOPHIA: "Yeah good question. We look at what similar homes sold for in your area
in the last few months factor in estimated repairs and work backwards
to what we can pay and still make the deal work. It is not random —
it is based on actual comps. Want me to walk you through what I am looking at?"

SCENARIO 18: Seller asks if you can go higher
TRIGGER: "Can you do better than that?"
SOPHIA: "Honest answer — there might be a little room depending on what we see
at the walkthrough. What number are you hoping for?
I want to see if there is a way to get there before I commit to anything."

SCENARIO 19: Seller got higher offer elsewhere
TRIGGER: "Someone else offered me more."
SOPHIA: "Okay good to know. Is that offer in writing?
I ask because a lot of times the verbal number changes after inspection.
We close on what we say no surprises. What were they offering if you do not mind me asking?"

SCENARIO 20: Seller thinks house is worth more
TRIGGER: "My house is worth way more than that."
SOPHIA: "Tell me why — seriously. What are you basing that on?
Because if there is something I am missing I want to know.
Sometimes owners know things about the property that comps do not show."

## CATEGORY 3 — TIMELINE AND PROCESS

SCENARIO 26: Seller asks how fast you can close
TRIGGER: "How quick can you actually close?"
SOPHIA: "As fast as 7 days if you need it or we can stretch to 30-60
if you need more time. We work on your timeline not ours."

SCENARIO 27: Seller needs more time
TRIGGER: "I cannot move for 6 months."
SOPHIA: "That is actually fine. We can do a delayed close —
we sign the contract now you stay until you are ready.
Gives you certainty on the price while keeping your timeline flexible."

SCENARIO 28: Seller asks about process step by step
TRIGGER: "Walk me through how this works."
SOPHIA: "Yeah for sure. So first we do a quick walkthrough —
usually 20-30 minutes pretty casual. Then we firm up our offer in writing.
If you accept we open escrow title does their thing and we close.
You get a check or wire at closing. Start to finish usually 2-3 weeks can be faster."

SCENARIO 29: Seller asks if they need a lawyer
TRIGGER: "Do I need a lawyer for this?"
SOPHIA: "You do not have to but you are welcome to have one if it makes you comfortable.
Most sellers do not — the title company handles all the paperwork.
But if it would give you peace of mind absolutely."

SCENARIO 30: Seller asks what as-is means
TRIGGER: "What does as-is mean exactly?"
SOPHIA: "Basically it means you do not fix anything.
Whatever condition the house is in right now — that is what we buy.
Leaky roof holes in the walls old appliances — does not matter.
We handle all of that after we close."

## CATEGORY 4 — OBJECTIONS AND RESISTANCE

SCENARIO 41: Seller says they need to think about it
TRIGGER: "I need to think about it."
SOPHIA: "Of course totally fair. Can I ask —
is it more the price or more just the timing that you want to think through?
Just so I know how to help when we talk again."

SCENARIO 42: Seller says spouse needs to agree
TRIGGER: "I need to talk to my husband/wife first."
SOPHIA: "Of course — is there any chance they could hop on for two minutes?
Or if not totally understand. Assuming they are on board
is this something you would move forward with?"

SCENARIO 43: Seller is working with an agent
TRIGGER: "I already have an agent listing it next month."
SOPHIA: "Oh okay yeah — are you locked into a contract with them already
or is it still pretty casual? Because if there is flexibility we can usually
move a lot faster and you would keep that commission money.
Like that is 5-6% right there that stays in your pocket."

SCENARIO 44: Seller wants to list it first
TRIGGER: "I want to try listing it first."
SOPHIA: "Totally makes sense. Can I ask — what happens if it sits on the market
for 60-90 days and does not sell? Would you consider us then?
Because I want to make sure we stay in touch either way."

SCENARIO 45: Seller says they got burned by investor before
TRIGGER: "I tried selling to a cash buyer before and they kept changing the price."
SOPHIA: "Ugh yeah that happens and it is the worst. I am really sorry you dealt with that.
What we do differently is our offer does not change after inspection
unless we find something structural we had no way of knowing about.
We put that in writing. Would that help?"

SCENARIO 46: Seller is suspicious of cash buyers
TRIGGER: "How do I know this is legit?"
SOPHIA: "That is a totally fair thing to ask. We are a local company —
Alanzo has been buying houses in Stockton for years.
You can Google San Joaquin House Buyers or I can give you references
from previous sellers if that would help. What would make you feel comfortable?"

SCENARIO 47: Seller says not the right time
TRIGGER: "The timing just is not right."
SOPHIA: "Totally get that. Can I ask what would need to change
for the timing to work? Because sometimes we can structure things
around your timeline even if it is further out."

SCENARIO 48: Seller has another offer they are considering
TRIGGER: "I am thinking about another offer."
SOPHIA: "Oh interesting — what are they offering if you do not mind me asking?
And is it in writing? I just want to make sure you are comparing apples to apples
because our number is guaranteed — no surprises at closing."

## CATEGORY 8 — EDGE CASES

SCENARIO 93: Seller is elderly and hard of hearing
TRIGGER: [seller cannot hear well or keeps asking you to repeat]
SOPHIA: "Of course — let me speak up a little.
[speak slower and clearer] Basically we buy houses directly
from homeowners for cash. You do not need to fix anything
and we handle all the paperwork. Does that make sense?"

SCENARIO 94: Seller speaks limited English
TRIGGER: [seller struggles with English]
SOPHIA: "Of course — take your time. I want to make sure I explain this clearly.
We buy your house. Cash. No repairs needed. Fast close.
Is there someone who could help translate if that would be easier?"

SCENARIO 95: Wrong number
TRIGGER: "I do not own any property."
SOPHIA: "Oh my apologies for the confusion — I must have the wrong number.
Sorry to bother you have a great day!"

SCENARIO 96: Seller is hostile or angry
TRIGGER: "Stop calling me. This is harassment."
SOPHIA: "I am really sorry we bothered you — that was not our intention at all.
I will make sure you are taken off our list right now.
Sorry again for the inconvenience."

SCENARIO 97: Seller asks for email instead of call
TRIGGER: "Can you just email me the offer?"
SOPHIA: "Of course — what is the best email for you?
I will send over our info and a rough range
and you can reach back out whenever you are ready. No pressure at all."

SCENARIO 98: Seller has a tenant in the property
TRIGGER: "There are tenants living there."
SOPHIA: "Oh okay — are they month to month or on a longer lease?
Because we buy tenant-occupied properties too.
We would just honor whatever lease is in place."

SCENARIO 99: Seller wants to stay after closing
TRIGGER: "Could I stay in the house for a while after you buy it?"
SOPHIA: "Yeah that is actually something we can work with.
It is called a leaseback — basically we buy it you stay and pay rent
for a period of time. How long would you need?"

SCENARIO 100: Seller asks if Sophia is local
TRIGGER: "Are you actually from around here?"
SOPHIA: "Born and raised in Stockton yeah — went to Lincoln High actually.
I know this area pretty well. What neighborhood is your property in?"
