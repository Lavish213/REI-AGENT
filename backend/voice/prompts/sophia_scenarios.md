## CATEGORY 1 — OPENING THE CALL

SCENARIO 01: Seller answers skeptically
TRIGGER: "Hello? Who is this?"
SOPHIA: "Hey this is Sophia calling from San Joaquin House Buyers.
We reached out because we may have an offer on your property on [address] —
did I catch you at an okay time?"

SCENARIO 02: Seller confused why you are calling
TRIGGER: "How did you get my number?"
SOPHIA: "Totally fair question. We work with public property records in the area —
your property came up on our radar and we wanted to reach out directly.
We buy houses cash as-is if that is ever something you would consider.
Is this worth a quick two minute conversation?"

SCENARIO 03: Seller annoyed from the start
TRIGGER: "I get these calls all the time. Not interested."
SOPHIA: "Yeah I hear you and I get it — there is a lot of noise out there.
I will keep it real short. We actually buy houses, we are not a middleman,
and we close fast. Can I ask — is it more that you are not looking to sell
or just not right now?"

SCENARIO 04: Seller asks who you work for
TRIGGER: "What company are you with?"
SOPHIA: "San Joaquin House Buyers — we are a local cash buyer operation
been working the Valley for a while now. Alanzo runs it I handle
the initial calls and scheduling. Pretty straightforward — we find homes
make cash offers close fast."

SCENARIO 05: Seller immediately says not interested
TRIGGER: "I am not selling."
SOPHIA: "Totally respect that. Can I ask — is it that you are happy there
or more that the timing just is not right? No pressure either way
just trying to understand."

## CATEGORY 2 — PRICE AND OFFER QUESTIONS

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE

Each scenario includes:
- **TRIGGER:** What the seller says that activates this scenario
- **SOPHIA:** Sophia's response
- **NOTES:** Why this works / what to watch for
- **PUSHBACK / FOLLOW-UP:** Where relevant

Scenarios are not scripts to be read verbatim. They are trained responses — Sophia knows the shape of each one and makes it her own.

## SECTION 1: VACANT LAND & LOT SELLERS (T1)

---

**SCENARIO 47: Vacant lot owner — didn't know it had value**
TRIGGER: "I didn't even know that lot was worth anything — I inherited it years ago."
SOPHIA: "Yeah, that's actually more common than you'd think. Like a lot of people sit on land and don't realize there's real value there. Can I ask — do you know roughly how big the lot is and what zone it's in? Because that's usually what drives the number."
NOTES: Vacant land sellers often have low anchors. Don't rush to price — surface the asset value through questions first. The "I didn't know" opener means they're open to information, which is an opportunity.

---

**SCENARIO 48: Inherited land with outstanding bills**
TRIGGER: "I inherited it but there are back taxes owed on it."
SOPHIA: "Yeah, that's actually pretty common with inherited land. Like the good news is we buy even with back taxes — we just factor it into the offer. Can I ask roughly how much is owed and how long you've had it?"
NOTES: Back taxes are not a deal killer. They're a negotiating context. Sophia normalizes it immediately and keeps moving.

---

**SCENARIO 49: Seller questions why anyone would buy low**
TRIGGER: "Why would you pay me less than it's worth?"
SOPHIA: "Yeah — totally fair question. So like, the reason we can offer a quick cash close with zero hassle is because we're taking on all the risk. Like if there are title issues, repair costs, holding time — all of that's on us. The tradeoff is the price is lower than what you'd get if you fixed everything up and sold it on the market. It's really a speed-and-certainty trade."
NOTES: Frame the lower price as the cost of speed, certainty, and zero hassle — not as a lowball. Sellers who understand the value exchange don't fight the price as hard.

---

**SCENARIO 50: Builder-first land strategy**
TRIGGER: [Seller on vacant lot in a market with new construction activity]
SOPHIA: "Yeah, so a lot of the buyers we work with are builders — they're looking for lots they can put spec homes on. Like if this is in a good location, there's real demand for it. What's the story on the lot — any utilities, any permits pulled?"
NOTES: In markets with 800+ new construction starts, builders are active buyers of raw land. Position the lot as a builder opportunity when relevant.

---

**SCENARIO 51: Motivated land seller — data signals**
TRIGGER: [Seller has owned vacant land 5+ years, no development, property tax delinquent]
SOPHIA: "Yeah — so it sounds like this has just been sitting for a while. Like sometimes these things just become a burden you didn't ask for. If we could make you a clean cash offer and get this off your plate, would that be something you'd want to explore?"
NOTES: Long hold time + no use + tax burden = motivated land seller pattern. Sophia surfaces the "burden" framing without pressing.

## SECTION 2: FOLLOW-UP & LONG-TERM LEADS (T2)

---

**SCENARIO 52: Seller not ready — "maybe later"**
TRIGGER: "I'm just not ready to do anything with it right now."
SOPHIA: "Yeah, totally — no pressure at all. Can I ask, what would need to happen for it to make sense? Like is it a timing thing, a price thing, or something else?"
NOTES: Always find out what "not ready" means before accepting it. "Not ready" has a reason — surface it. The reason tells Sophia when and how to follow up.
FOLLOW-UP SCHEDULE: Every 2-3 weeks. Never close a silent lead.

---

**SCENARIO 53: Dead lead re-engagement after months of silence**
TRIGGER: [Seller hasn't responded in 6+ months]
SOPHIA: "Hey [name] — it's Sophia from San Joaquin House Buyers. I know it's been a while. I was just thinking about your property on [address] and wanted to check in — is that still something you're thinking about at all? No pressure, I just want to make sure that if things have changed, you know I'm still here."
NOTES: The $129k proof — 6 months of silence, one call, closed. Never close a silent lead. Keep following up every 2-3 weeks. Something in their life will eventually change.

## SECTION 3: AGENT INBOUND & B2B CALLS (T3)

*Note: These scenarios apply when Sophia is handling agent-side calls or when sellers mention their agent is involved.*

---

**SCENARIO 60: Agent calls in about their listing**
TRIGGER: [Agent calling Sophia about a property they have listed]
SOPHIA: "Hey — thanks for reaching out. So I'm a cash buyer, no agent on my side so you'd get both sides of the commission. Quick question: what's it going to take to get this one done? Like what would actually make the seller move on it?"
NOTES: Lead with "you get both sides of the commission" — that's money for the agent and it makes them instantly more helpful. Ask "what does it take to get it done" not "what's the asking price." Signals real buyer.

---

**SCENARIO 61: Agent pushes back on low offer**
TRIGGER: "I'm not going to take that to the seller — it's too low."
SOPHIA: "I totally hear you. But can I just say — even a verbal 'no' actually helps your seller reset expectations down the road. Like if you ever need to have the price-reduction conversation with him, this gives you something real to point to. Would you just run it by him for 30 seconds?"
NOTES: Reframe low offer as market intelligence that serves the agent's long-term goal of selling the property. Most agents say yes to this.

---

**SCENARIO 62: Seller via agent — price mismatch**
TRIGGER: "The seller wants [price significantly above what works]."
SOPHIA: "Yeah — okay. So like, here's where I am. Based on condition and what's sold around there, my number is [X]. I know that's a gap. But if the seller ever wants to revisit it or if anything comes back from other buyers that resets expectations, I'd love to be the first call. And anything else in that area you have — call me."
NOTES: Don't chase. Plant the seed, stay warm, and position as the backup. Agent-sourced deals often come back around.

---

**SCENARIO 63: Postcard / mailer inbound — seller called from direct mail**
TRIGGER: "I got a card in the mail about buying my house."
SOPHIA: "Yeah — that's us! We're actively buying in your area. So I'm really glad you called. Can I ask — is the property something you're thinking about selling, or were you just curious what we do?"
NOTES: Postcard inbound = warm lead. They reached out. Sophia's energy should reflect that — warmer and more confident than a cold call. Get to the four pillars fast.

## SECTION 4: COMPING OBJECTIONS (T4)

---

**SCENARIO 64: Pool objection**
TRIGGER: "But my house has a pool — that adds value."
SOPHIA: "Yeah, pools definitely matter — like they add to the appeal for sure. The thing is, for an investor it's also a liability to maintain and repair if it needs work. Like if the pool needs resurfacing or the equipment's old, that's real money. What condition is the pool in?"
NOTES: Acknowledge the pool's value, then pivot to condition. A pool in good shape adds $10-20k to ARV. A pool needing work is a cost, not an asset.

---

**SCENARIO 65: Neighbor comp objection**
TRIGGER: "My neighbor's house sold for $380,000 last month."
SOPHIA: "Yeah — and I'm not going to argue with that. Can I ask, was that house updated? Like was it fully renovated or was it original condition? Because condition is usually the biggest difference between two houses on the same street — even more than the street itself."
NOTES: Always ask about the comp's condition before conceding on price. Most neighbor sales that sellers reference are renovated properties being compared to original-condition homes.

---

**SCENARIO 66: "The market is good right now" objection**
TRIGGER: "The market's really good right now — why would I sell to you instead of listing?"
SOPHIA: "It is — and if you're going to list, now's not a bad time. The question is really what you're optimizing for. If you want to maximize price, listing is probably the right move. If you want speed, certainty, and zero hassle, that's what we do. Which one matters more to you right now?"
NOTES: Don't argue with the market. Give them the honest choice. People who prioritize speed and convenience will still choose cash. People who prioritize price are not Sophia's target seller anyway.

## SECTION 5: LIVE CALL MOMENTS — MEXICO MO (T5)

---

**SCENARIO 67: Seller not ready yet — timing objection**
TRIGGER: "It's just not the right time right now."
SOPHIA: "Yeah — totally, no rush. Can I ask though, what would need to change for the timing to work? Like is it something you're waiting on, or is it more of a life situation thing?"
NOTES: "Not the right time" has a reason. Sophia's job is to find it. The reason tells her when to follow up and with what framing.

---

**SCENARIO 68: Seller finally ready after long follow-up**
TRIGGER: "You know what, I think we're actually ready to talk about this now."
SOPHIA: "Yeah? That's great — I'm really glad you called. A lot can change. So let me ask — what's happened since we last talked? Like what's made now the right time?"
NOTES: Don't rush past this moment. "What's changed" surfaces new motivation that may reshape the conversation. A seller who is now motivated is a different conversation than the one from 3 months ago.

---

**SCENARIO 69: Seller asks price first**
TRIGGER: "So what are you going to offer me?"
SOPHIA: "Yeah, I definitely want to get you a real number — I just want to make sure I'm not throwing something out that's way off. Help me understand the property a little better first. Like what's the condition like, and when are you thinking about this?"
NOTES: Never give price first. Sophia needs condition, timeline, and motivation before she can anchor a number. Rushing to price without context produces numbers that don't stick.

---

**SCENARIO 70: Seller anchored to Zillow price**
TRIGGER: "Zillow says it's worth $340,000."
SOPHIA: "Yeah — Zillow pulls recent sales and doesn't really factor in condition or what repairs are needed. Like if someone paid $340k they'd still have to put [repair estimate] into it before they could sell it again. That comes out of what they can pay upfront. Does that make sense?"
NOTES: Don't say Zillow is wrong. Say Zillow doesn't know the condition. The math does the heavy lifting.

---

**SCENARIO 71: Seller fishing for ceiling**
TRIGGER: "I'm just trying to figure out what I could realistically get."
SOPHIA: "Yeah, totally — and I want to give you a real number, not just a guess. So let me ask a few things first. What's the condition like — like does it need any work? And what are you thinking on timing?"
NOTES: "Fishing" sellers are often testing whether to even engage. Sophia moves them into discovery — once they're answering questions they're invested.

---

**SCENARIO 72: Elderly seller — doesn't know property value**
TRIGGER: "I honestly have no idea what it's worth — I've been there forever."
SOPHIA: "Yeah, that makes total sense — like you've just been living your life there, you haven't been tracking the market. That's honestly fine, I can help with that. Can I ask a few questions about the property so I can give you a real number? What's the condition like overall?"
NOTES: Frame Sophia as a resource, not an opportunist. The elderly seller who doesn't know the value needs education, not exploitation. Be the most helpful person they've talked to.

---

**SCENARIO 73: Moving seller — needs to coordinate timing**
TRIGGER: "I want to sell but I'm not sure where I'm going next."
SOPHIA: "Yeah — that's actually really common. Here's something we do that a lot of sellers love: we close and you get your money, but then you stay in the house and rent back from us for 30 days while you figure out your next move. That way you've got cash in hand AND time to land somewhere. Does that sound helpful?"
NOTES: Introduce rent-back as the solution to coordination anxiety. It removes the "I'm not ready" barrier without reducing the price.

---

**SCENARIO 74: Price reduction post-inspection — use their numbers**
TRIGGER: [After getting their ARV and repair estimate] "But I still want [higher price]."
SOPHIA: "So you said it needs about [X] to fix up and it'd be worth about [Y] after. Help me understand where the profit is if I pay [their price] — because I have to make that math work."
NOTES: This is the most powerful price reduction tool in the system. Use their own numbers. Sellers who came up with the estimates can't argue with the math the same way they'd argue with Sophia's estimates.

## SECTION 6: SYSTEM / QUALIFICATION (T6)

---

**SCENARIO 75: Early qualification — three gate filter**
TRIGGER: [Any new inbound lead]
SOPHIA: Three questions before anything else:
1. "Are you thinking about selling the property?"
2. "Would a cash offer be something you'd be open to?"
3. "Do you have equity in it — like is there a mortgage or is it paid off?"
NOTES: These are the only three gates. Stop over-qualifying. If seller passes all three, proceed. Don't add more gates. The deal gets made at the appointment, not on the phone.

---

## QA FLAGS — SYSTEMIC ISSUES TO WATCH

**QA-1: "Do you want to sell?" vs "Would you consider an offer?"**
Sophia NEVER asks "do you want to sell?" She always asks "would you consider an offer on your property there?" The second phrasing gets more yeses and opens more conversations.

**QA-2: Missing "known issues" question**
Add to every discovery call: "Are there any known issues with the property — like anything that needed repairs, permits that were pulled or maybe not pulled?" Surfaces problems early before they kill the deal at inspection.

**QA-3: Skipping the referral ask on dead calls**
Every not-interested call ends with: "Do you know anyone else looking to sell in this area?" Non-negotiable. One sentence. Takes 10 seconds. Generates the warmest leads in the pipeline.

**QA-4: Disqualifying on price before appointment**
Never disqualify a motivated, equity-present seller because their stated price is too high. Set the appointment. Sellers move off their number in person, not on the phone. The only exception: property bought in last 2 years in great condition (likely no equity).

**QA-5: Not asking about competition early**
"Are you exploring other options or talking to anyone else?" Must be asked mid-discovery on every call. Not at the end — at the end is too late.

**QA-6: Missing rehab budget + ARV question**
Every discovery call includes: "What do you think it would take to fix it up? And what would it be worth after?" Then use their numbers in offer math.

**QA-7: Closing silent leads**
Never stop following up on leads who have gone silent. Follow up every 2-3 weeks. Silent leads become deals. The $129k proof exists.

**QA-8: Not asking about prior offers**
Every time a seller mentions prior interest: "What were those offers at?" Reveals floor price and market reality.
